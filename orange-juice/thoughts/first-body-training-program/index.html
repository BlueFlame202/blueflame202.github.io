I was on hiatus from this for a while because I was trying to go all out in the fall, taking excessive amounts of coursework, IM sports, research, grad apps, etc., which was fun and definitely an experience.

Now I'm back. I'm home right now, so I'm going to focus on calisthenics and record a diary here. Since that's a lot of work, I'm going to stick to minimal entries rather than my older style below which was nice but not really sustainable.

June 13, 2025, 9:30 AM PST

I decided to make a quick update since it's been some time! I've been seeing some pretty cool advancements in my weight-lifting abilities! The first week, I failed to finish 5 sets of 5 on bench press with 95 pounds, but now I hit 5x5 with 100 pounds yesterday! I also feel much more comfortable doing squats and deadlifts with the barbell, and I think my dumbell overhead press has gotten better.

That being said, my shoulder has been hurting a lot more than usual, so I've been taking things a bit slow (so the missing days on my sheet below are less of a consistency issue). Hopefully that will get better soon. Oddly, the pain hasn't actually been impacting my workouts too much... for some reason I even feel that the pain gets better during my workouts, and I only notice it when I'm not working out. I'm not sure if this is a good sign or a bad sign.

So far I've been seeing strength improvements, which I'm happy about! Can't wait to reach a plate in a few weeks!

My food habits haven't been amazing, so I think I could probably be getting better gains. This is something I'll try to work on in the coming weeks.

June 1, 2025, 10:48 PM PST

I've tried to get into weight lifting or calisthenics a few times before, but never really got into it. This time, I'm trying to be more serious about it.

The first problem for me is what to do. I have a few go to exercises that I enjoy:

  • Bench press
  • Leg press (I never really enjoyed the squat rack because I can't figure out how to place the bar on my back without my neck hurting significantly afterwards)
  • Machines
but to be honest, I've never really locked in a good program and stuck to it. Being too afraid to try deadlifts and overhead presses was also a major problem.

A friend of mine recommended StrongLifts 5x5, but I've seen mixed reviews. I'm a little skeptical since he claimed he tried it after he was already into weight lifting, which is not the case for me. StrongLifts tends to advertise itself as a program for beginners, but I've also seen pretty mixed reviews on Reddit, with some people going as far as to say that Mehdi doesn't know what he's talking about. I feel the same about nsuns, and many programs that I've heard good things about are paid.

In spite of my skepticism, I'll probably start with StrongLifts 5x5 and see how it goes. Learning without making any mistakes would be ideal but feels pretty inorganic anyway.

For my nutrition plan, I'm aiming to eat roughly 3000 calories a day, which is more than before. I'm trying to eat a lot of carbs and protein for dinner after an evening workout.

To stay more disciplined, I'm going to track my progress in three ways:

  1. The StrongLifts 5x5 Spreadsheet (New lifter tab)
  2. Cronometer for nutrition tracking
  3. This diary
Looking forward to it!
